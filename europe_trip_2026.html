Eat Your Way Across Europe

Food & Drink Highlights

🇸🇪 Stockholm

Sweden
  • ☕
    Fika
    Coffee + cinnamon bun (kanelbulle) — a Swedish institution. Try Café Saturnus for legendary buns.
  • 🥣
    Swedish Meatballs
    Authentic version at Meatballs for the People (Nytorget). Much better than IKEA!
  • 🐟
    Smörgåsbord
    Traditional Swedish spread with herring, gravlax, and rye bread at Operakällaren.
  • 🍺
    Glögg
    Hot spiced mulled wine at the Christmas markets — get a non-alcoholic version for your 12-year-old.

❄️ Kiruna

Swedish Lapland
  • 🦌
    Reindeer
    Try reindeer stew or smoked reindeer — it's a Sami staple and delicious. Available at most local restaurants.
  • 🫐
    Cloudberry jam
    Golden Arctic berries — try on pancakes or with cheese. Unique to the region.
  • 🍵
    Hot lingonberry juice
    After dog sledding, this warming drink is perfect for all ages.

🇩🇰 Copenhagen

Denmark
  • 🥐
    Danish Pastry
    Try a real wienerbrød at Hart Bageri (owned by a Noma chef). Worth the queue.
  • 🌭
    Pølser (Hot dogs)
    Danish street hot dogs from a pølsevogn cart. Kids love them, and they're cheap!
  • 🍻
    Torvehallerne Market
    Fantastic covered food market — great for a lunch with lots of variety. Easy family option.
  • 🦞
    Smørrebrød
    Open-faced rye bread sandwiches with elaborate toppings. Try Aamanns for a modern take.

🇩🇪 Munich

Bavaria · Christmas Eve
  • 🥨
    Brezel
    Soft pretzels from any bakery — best eaten warm with butter. Perfect market snack.
  • 🐷
    Schweinshaxe
    Crispy pork knuckle — a Bavarian classic. Try at Haxnbauer or Augustiner-Keller.
  • 🌭
    Weisswurst
    White sausage with sweet mustard — a Christmas morning tradition. Pair with Weissbier.
  • 🎂
    Christmas Eve Dinner
    Book in advance! Spatenhaus an der Oper is great for a festive Bavarian Christmas dinner.

🎿 Innsbruck

Austria · Alps
  • 🧀
    Käsespätzle
    Alpine mac-and-cheese — a total kid favourite. Available at every mountain hut and tavern.
  • 🍩
    Kaiserschmarrn
    Fluffy shredded pancakes with plum sauce — the best après-ski dessert. Try at a mountain hut.
  • 🍵
    Glühwein (Punsch for kids)
    Warm punch at ski-slope bars. Kids get the non-alcoholic Kinderpunsch.

🇫🇷 Paris

France · NYE
  • 🥐
    Croissants & Pain au chocolat
    Go to Du Pain et des Idées in Le Marais — possibly the best in Paris, and near your hotel!
  • 🧅
    French Onion Soup
    Warming winter classic — try at Au Pied de Cochon (open 24h, great for NYE after midnight).
  • 🍫
    Angelina hot chocolate
    The thick African hot chocolate at Angelina on Rue de Rivoli is legendary. A must for the family.
  • 🎆
    NYE Dinner
    Book at least 2 months ahead. L'Ami Jean in the 7th is excellent value for a festive meal.

🇧🇪 Bruges

Belgium
  • 🍺
    Belgian Beer
    Try a Bruges Zot at the De Halve Maan Brewery — they also do a family brewery tour!
  • 🍟
    Frites
    Belgian fries with mayonnaise from Frituur No. 1 near the Belfry. Eat them in a paper cone.
  • 🍫
    Chocolate
    Visit The Chocolate Line or Dumon for handmade truffles. Your 12-year-old will love picking their own box.
  • 🧇
    Belgian Waffles
    Liège-style (chewy, caramelised) beats Brussels-style (crispy) in winter. Get them from a street vendor.

🇬🇧 London

United Kingdom
  • 🐟
    Fish & Chips
    Rock & Sole Plaice in Covent Garden is a classic. Eat with mushy peas and malt vinegar.
  • ☕
    Full English Breakfast
    Fuelling up before a big sightseeing day — find a proper "greasy spoon" caff for the full experience.
  • 🎭
    Pre-theatre dinner
    J. Sheekey near the West End for pre-show dining — book in advance alongside your show tickets.
  • 🍺
    Pub meal
    A proper British pub with a Sunday roast — The Anchor at Bankside has great views of St. Paul's.

💡 Train Booking Tips

  • 🎫 Book European trains 3 months ahead for best prices — especially Christmas/NYE period
  • 👶 Children under 15 travel free on most European trains when accompanied by an adult
  • 🇪🇺 Consider an Interrail Pass if not already booked — 3 adults (2 adult + 1 youth) for the 5 train legs

Expert Advice

Top Tips for Your Trip

🌌 Northern Lights

Kiruna Dec 17–19 is excellent timing. The lights are best seen between 10pm–2am, away from light pollution. Book a guided tour for the best experience — guides know the best viewing spots. Clear skies are key, so have a backup night planned. Aurora apps like "My Aurora Forecast" help track activity.

👧 Travelling with a 12-Year-Old

Your child is at a great age for this trip — old enough to enjoy museums and culture, young enough to be wowed by snow and lights. Involve them in planning; let them pick one activity per city. Give them their own small spending budget for souvenirs — builds independence. Pack a travel journal for them.

💳 Money & Cards

Scandinavia is nearly cashless — cards everywhere. Germany and Austria still use some cash (carry €50–100). Paris accepts cards widely. Bruges and London: cards fine. Get a Wise or Revolut travel card to avoid foreign transaction fees — could save you $300+ over the trip.

🎄 Christmas in Europe

Many shops and restaurants close Dec 24–26, especially in Munich and Innsbruck. Stock up on snacks the day before. Christmas markets in Stockholm and Copenhagen close Dec 23–24. The Tivoli Gardens in Copenhagen is magical at Christmas — try to go on the evening of Dec 20 upon arrival.

🚆 Train Travel Tips

Book trains 3 months in advance for Christmas period pricing. Seat reservations are mandatory on TGV (France) and some ICE (Germany) trains. Kids under 15 often travel free with a paying adult on many European networks. Always have a digital and printed backup of your tickets.

❄️ Cold Weather Prep

Kiruna in December averages -15°C to -20°C. Dress in layers and cover all exposed skin. Hand warmers are your best friend for night photography and Northern Lights tours. Batteries drain fast in cold — keep phones and cameras in inner pockets. Sunscreen still needed (UV reflects off snow).

🎉 New Year's Eve in Paris

The Champs-Élysées NYE is free and spectacular. Arrive well before midnight — by 9pm for a decent position near the Arc de Triomphe. The metro runs all night NYE for free. Restaurants charge premium NYE menus — book 2–3 months ahead. Consider having dinner early and joining the street party later.

🎿 Skiing Tips for Beginners

Axamer Lizum is a great choice for mixed-ability families. Book lessons in advance (post-Christmas is busy). Hire equipment at the resort — cheaper than dragging gear from Australia. Start with a 2-hour group lesson for beginners, then upgrade to private if confidence grows. Helmets are compulsory for under-14s in Austria.

📸 Photography

Winter light in Scandinavia is beautiful — golden hour lasts hours at this latitude. For Northern Lights: use a tripod, ISO 1600–3200, aperture f/2.8, shutter 10–15 seconds. In cities, early morning is magical before crowds arrive — especially Bruges canals and Paris streets. Gamla Stan in Stockholm looks incredible with snow.

🏥 Health & Safety

EU countries are covered by EHIC (if you hold one) but don't rely on it — get comprehensive travel insurance. In Kiruna, frostbite can occur within 30 minutes at -20°C without proper gear. Keep travel insurance documents on your phone and email. Emergency number throughout Europe: 112.

🗺️ City Cards

Consider: Stockholm City Card (museums + transport), Copenhagen Card (80+ attractions + metro), Munich City Card (transport only). London is pricey for attractions — book Tower of London and London Eye in advance online for 10–15% discount. British Museum and National Gallery are free!

🌐 Language Tips

Swedes, Danes, and Dutch all speak excellent English. In Germany and Austria, basic German phrases are appreciated. In Paris, always try "Bonjour!" first — the French warm up considerably. In London, obviously no language barrier, but "please" and "cheers" go a long way. Download Google Translate offline packs for all countries.
